# Practical Info

No hire car — everything runs on the coastal train, a couple of buses, and walking. Menton is the still point; Nice, Monaco, Ventimiglia and Dolceacqua are all short hops on the line.

> 💷 **FX:** £1 ≈ €1.16 (ECB, 26 Jun 2026). All prices £ first, € in brackets.

---

## ✈️ Getting in and out

> ⚠️ **Flight change:** original BA8475 (09:45) cancelled → rebooked **BA336**.

- **In:** **BA336** from London **Heathrow T5**, lands Nice (NCE) Terminal 1 **20:40** Sat 27 Jun (a late arrival — straight to Menton, no Nice tonight)
- **Out:** BA8468 NCE → London **City** (LCY), departs **17:00** Sun 5 Jul (T1, check-in closes 16:00)
- ⚠️ **Different London airports each way** (Heathrow out, City back). **BA8468 punctuality is poor** (avg ~51-min delay) — keep the return buffer comfortable

## 🚖 Arrival night (Sat 27 Jun) — getting to Menton after 20:40

- **Taxi / Uber direct (recommended):** ~35 min, **~£55–65 (€65–75)**. No-faff after a travel day with bags. Rank outside T1
- ⚠️ **Express 80 bus stops ~19:40** — not running this late
- **Cheaper fallback:** Tram T2 into Nice (to ~00:35) + last Sat TER Nice→Menton (~23:36) — doable but a midnight slog with luggage

## 🚆 Airport ↔ Nice ↔ Menton

- **Tram T2** NCE → Nice centre (~30 min). **AERO ticket £8.60 (€10) return pp** — also covers the Sun 5 Jul return leg
- **TER coastal train** Nice-Ville → Menton: ~35–40 min, **£7 (€8) pp**, ~every 20–30 min
- Alight at **Gare de Menton (main)** — NOT Menton-Garavan. ~5–10 min walk to 28 Ave de la Riviera through Jardin Biovès
- Tickets at station machines (card/coin) or the SNCF Connect app

## 🚌 Excursion transport

- **Menton ↔ Nice-Ville:** TER ~35–40 min, ~£7 (€8)
- **Nice ↔ Monaco-Monte-Carlo:** TER ~20 min, ~£4.30 (€5)
- **Menton ↔ Monaco-Monte-Carlo:** TER ~12 min, ~£3.40 (€4)
- **Menton ↔ Ventimiglia:** TER ~16 min, ~£2.60 (€3) — **carry passport** (occasional border ID checks)
- **Ventimiglia ↔ Dolceacqua:** Riviera Trasporti **Bus 7** ~13 min, €2–3; catch on Via Cavour ~7 min from Ventimiglia station. Saturday = weekday ("feriale") service, so it runs

## 📱 Mobile data — Free SIM

- Sorting a **Free** SIM (high-data backup if the Airbnb WiFi is weak) on Sunday morning in Nice
- **Free self-service borne** = SIM in ~3 min on a **UK card, no French bank account** (Free's tourist quirk)
- Central Nice bornes: Fnac La Riviera (44–46 av Jean Médecin), Maison de la Presse (35 av Jean Médecin / 126 rue de France). Staffed Free shops are **closed Sundays** — borne only
- **Fallback: CAP3000 mall** (one TER stop short of Nice), staffed Free boutique open **Sun 11:00–19:00**
- Forfait: Free 5G, ~€20/mo, 300 GB, no commitment

## 🏖️ Beaches & swimming

- Closest beaches (Borrigo/Casino/Marché, ~1.3 km) are **rocky** — bring water shoes
- **Fossan (1.7 km)** or **Sablettes (2.1 km, sandy)** are the better swim spots
- **Lifeguards July–August only** — late-June early/late swims are unsupervised; check the day's flags when surveillance starts (~1 July)

---

## 💶 Money & costs

- **Arrival taxi** ~£55–65 is the one chunky transport cost (late landing, straight to base)
- **Monaco** is a tourist-price trap — keep it light: the F1 afternoon is cheap (Car Collection £13, circuit walk free), just an aperitivo by the port
- **Italy** is good value — the Ligurian lunch at Casa e Bottega is quality without a blowout
- Everyday Menton dinners run £9–34pp depending on the spot
- Carry some cash for market socca stalls and bus tickets

---

## 📌 Day-of checklist

- Re-pull TER + bus times the morning of each day out (SNCF Connect; Riviera Trasporti site)
- **Re-confirm BA336 (out, 27 Jun) and BA8468 (return, 5 Jul) 24h before**
- Confirm a central Nice Free borne host is open Sun morning (else CAP3000, open Sun 11:00–19:00)
- Check the Fête de la Saint-Pierre programme week-of (4–5 July; schedule can shift slightly)

## ☀️ Season notes

- Late June / early July: warm, long evenings, sea swimmable. Pack for heat + sun
- The two free days carry the trip — keep them for the excursions; weekday evenings stay near base
- One splurge dinner (L'Orangerie) is sanctioned; everything else value-led
